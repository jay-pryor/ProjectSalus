# QinetiQ - Counter-UAS Products and Specifications

## Company Overview

QinetiQ is a UK-headquartered defence technology company that has been researching aerial threats and developing counter-drone capability for over a decade. Their primary counter-UAS offering is the **Obsidian** system, purpose-built for drone detection from the ground up.

QinetiQ believes radar is the most threat-agnostic drone sensor, requiring no specific communications signals to intercept and no threat library maintenance.

---

## Obsidian Counter-Drone System

The Obsidian radar was developed following a comprehensive threat analysis research programme. Its technology derives from the ALARM system used by the British Army to provide warning of impending aerial attacks. Obsidian is specifically designed to detect, identify, and track small UAS threats in complex environments including airports, military installations, and critical national infrastructure.

### Radar Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Radar Type** | X-Band FMCW (Frequency Modulated Continuous Wave) |
| **Frequency Band** | X-Band (optimised for detecting small motor/rotor blade signatures) |
| **Detection Range (UAVs < 20 kg)** | Up to 2 km (firmware configurable) |
| **Extended Detection Range** | Up to 3.5 km (firmware configurable for larger UAVs) |
| **Coverage (paired radars)** | 360 degrees azimuth, 90 degrees elevation |
| **Detection Bubble (paired)** | Up to 7 km diameter |
| **Minimum Detection Range** | 20 m |
| **Minimum Detectable Velocity** | 0.5 m/s |
| **Update Rate** | 0.5 seconds |
| **Simultaneous Targets** | 100+ |
| **Antenna Type** | Staring array (80+ static antenna beams, no spinning antenna or electronic scanning) |

### Physical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Dimensions (single radar unit)** | 80 cm (W) x 49 cm (D) x 56.5 cm (H) |
| **Weight (single radar unit)** | < 45 kg |
| **Power Requirements** | Not publicly disclosed |

### Environmental Operating Conditions

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Minimum Steady-State Operating Temperature** | -46 degrees C |
| **Minimum Cold Start Temperature** | -40 degrees C |
| **Cold Start Behaviour** | Internal heaters activate to warm the radar interior |

### Standard Package Components

- 2x 180-degree Obsidian radar units (back-to-back for 360-degree coverage)
- Tracker unit
- PTZ camera (multiple options available)
- Command and Control (C2) interface with two operator screens

### Detection and Classification Capabilities

- **Micro-Doppler Detection**: Uses micro-Doppler radar techniques to detect drone rotor blade signatures, enabling detection even when a drone is hovering or stationary.
- **AI-Enabled Classification**: Advanced AI applied to both radar returns and camera imagery to distinguish drones from birds and other objects, minimising false alarms.
- **Target Types**: Drones, paragliders, parachutes, and other small aerial objects.
- **3D Accuracy**: High-accuracy 3D position information for all tracked targets.

### Command and Control (C2) System

- Map-based user interface displaying track data
- User-definable exclusion zones and alert/prohibit zones in 3 dimensions
- User-selectable data layers
- Integration with leading effector systems to trigger defensive actions

### Mobility and Deployment Modes

- **Static**: Fixed installation for airfields, bases, and critical infrastructure
- **Transportable**: Rapid deployment to reported disruption areas
- **On-the-Move (OTM)**: Vehicle-mounted operation from static or moving platforms (land or sea)
- **Convoy Protection**: Full aerial situational awareness from mobile platforms

### Integration

- Integrates with leading effector systems (jammers, kinetic effectors)
- Extensible through additional sensors: supplementary radars, RF sensing, Direction Finding (DF) systems
- Multi-sensor fusion with EO/IR cameras for visual confirmation
- Unified network interface for linking multiple Obsidian deployments across locations

## Deployment History and Use Cases

- Trialed at Ottawa Macdonald-Cartier International Airport (YOW), Canada
- In service across North America, Europe, and the UK
- Designed for airfield protection, military operations, and critical national infrastructure
- Suitable for force protection, convoy protection, and rapid response scenarios

## Relevance to Land 156

While QinetiQ's Obsidian is a prominent counter-drone radar product, publicly available information does not confirm a direct QinetiQ contract under Australia's Land 156 programme. Companies explicitly named in Land 156 activities include DroneShield, EOS, and Silentium Defence. However, Obsidian's capabilities (mobile/static deployment, AI classification, multi-sensor integration) align closely with Land 156 requirements for an integrated counter-UAS system.
