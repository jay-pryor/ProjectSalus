# HENSOLDT Products and Specifications

## Company Overview

HENSOLDT is a German multinational defence electronics company headquartered in Taufkirchen, Germany. The company specialises in radar, electronic warfare, optronics, and avionics systems. HENSOLDT's product lines are highly relevant to counter-UAS operations, ground-based air defence (GBAD), border surveillance, and critical infrastructure protection.

### HENSOLDT Australia

- **Entity:** HENSOLDT Australia Pty Ltd (formerly IE Asia-Pacific Pty Ltd, founded 2009)
- **Headquarters:** Unit 4, 50 Collie St, Fyshwick, ACT 2609
- **Additional Offices:** Adelaide, Hobart, Newcastle
- **Capabilities:** Design and installation of radars, electronic solutions, electro-optronics, air traffic control systems, integrated bridge systems, hydrogen systems, and through-life support services
- **Recognition:** Winner of the Prime Minister's Award for Medium Business of the Year (2021)

### Relevance to Australian Land 156 Program

As of early 2026, HENSOLDT is **not** among the announced prime contractors or subcontractors for Australia's Project Land 156 counter-UAS program. Leidos Australia was selected as the systems integration partner (AU$41M contract), with Australian partners including Acacia Systems (Cortex C2), EOS Defence Systems (effectors), and Department 13 (sensors). However, HENSOLDT's counter-UAS product portfolio (particularly Elysion C-UAS / Xpeller and the Spexer radar family) represents a competitive offering that could be relevant to future Land 156 phases or adjacent programs. Australia has committed approximately A$1.3 billion over the next decade for layered counter-drone architecture.

## 1. Spexer 2000 3D MkIII (Land & Naval Variants)

### Overview

The Spexer 2000 3D MkIII is HENSOLDT's flagship short-range 3D surveillance radar, designed for battlefield surveillance, counter-UAS, and very short-range air defence (VSHORAD). It uses AESA-based X-band pulse-Doppler technology with superior subclutter visibility. Available in both Land and Naval variants. HENSOLDT is tripling production capacity at a new radar test site in Germany to meet surging demand, including deliveries to Ukraine alongside the TRML-4D.

### Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Frequency Band** | X-band (9.2 - 10 GHz) |
| **Bandwidth** | 15 MHz at operational frequency |
| **Channels** | >100 |
| **Radar Type** | AESA, full-coherent pulse-Doppler |
| **Azimuth Coverage** | 120 degrees (per panel); 360 degrees with 4 panels |
| **Elevation Coverage** | 90 degrees (per panel) |
| **Simultaneous Beams** | Up to 16 |
| **Maximum Instrumented Range** | 40 km (single beam); 2.5 km (16 simultaneous beams) |
| **Update Rate** | 0.3 seconds (fastest) to 7.5 seconds (slow scan) |
| **Tracking Capacity** | >300 targets per 120-degree sector |
| **Scan on the Move** | Yes |

### Detection Ranges by Target Type

| Target | RCS (m^2) | Detection Range |
|---|---|---|
| Pedestrian | 0.5 | ~18-20 km |
| Small UAV | 0.2 | ~9 km |
| Small Boat | 1.5 | ~20 km |
| Light Vehicle | 2.0 | ~22 km |
| Light Aircraft | 3.0 | ~27 km |
| Low-Flying Aircraft | 5.0 | ~36 km |

### Size, Weight and Power (SWaP)

| Component | Dimensions (mm) | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| AESA Antenna (MkIII) | 600 x 500 x 200 | 35 kg |
| AESA Antenna (previous gen) | 1,000 x 700 x 200 | 73 kg |
| GPU (RFU + Radar Processor) | 600 x 400 x 300 | 40 kg |

- Power consumption: Not publicly specified, but described as significantly reduced compared to previous generation, enabling installation on a wider range of vehicles.

### Key Features

- Automatic detection and classification of small, slow, and fast targets including UAVs, USVs, projectiles/missiles, RAM, sea skimmers, and rubber dinghies
- On-the-move capability for both land and maritime platforms
- Naval variant optimised for asymmetric maritime threats (small fast boats, jet skis, sea-skimming missiles)

## 2. Spexer 600

### Overview

The Spexer 600 is a multi-mission, X-band ground-based surveillance radar developed by HENSOLDT UK. It utilises SharpEye solid-state transceiver technology with an AESA antenna, designed for battlefield surveillance, border security, drone detection, and ISTAR missions.

### Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Frequency Band** | X-band |
| **Antenna Technology** | AESA with SharpEye solid-state transceiver |
| **Coverage** | 120-degree arc |
| **Tracking Capacity** | >500 tracks (Track While Scan) |
| **MTBF** | >30,000 hours |
| **Detection Range (walking person)** | ~6 km (estimated from naming convention) |
| **Signal Processing** | SharpEye patented pulse sequencing with built-in tracker/plot extractor |
| **Automatic Classification** | Yes, in all modes |
| **Military Standard** | Ruggedised to Mil-Std |

### Deployment Options

- Tripod, vehicle, mast, or building-mounted
- Lightweight, crew-portable; can be set up in minutes
- Low weight allows installation without specialist masts or structures
- Controlled locally or remotely from a command centre
- Easily networked with other sensors; electro-optics agnostic

### Key Features

- Graceful degradation under damage
- Low life cycle cost
- Built-in tracker reduces need for additional electronics units
- Designed for military, paramilitary, and critical infrastructure applications

## 5. XPELLER Counter-UAS System

### Overview

Xpeller is HENSOLDT's modular counter-UAV system employing a multi-sensor/effector approach based on a four-step workflow: **Detect, Identify, Control, and Act**. It combines HENSOLDT's own radars, IR cameras, and jammers with partner products (RF detectors, acoustic sensors). The name derives from "expelling an intruder."

### System Components

| Component | Product | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Radar** | Spexer 500 / Spexer 2000 3D | 120-degree azimuth, 30-degree elevation; 4W RF output |
| **Thermal Camera** | Z:NightOwl ZM-ER | Long-range thermal imaging; purpose-designed for high range |
| **Jammer** | UAV Jammer | 20 MHz - 6,000 MHz; directional and omnidirectional variants |
| **RF Detectors** | myDefence (partner) | Close-in RF detection |
| **Direction Finders** | Skyscan 7x | RF direction finding |
| **Acoustic Sensors** | Squarehead "Discovair" (partner) | Drone acoustic signature detection; range of several hundred metres |
| **C2 Software** | HENSOLDT proprietary | Multi-sensor fusion, intuitive map-based situational awareness |

### Performance

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Detection Range (UAV)** | ~4 km (typical); varies by drone type |
| **Jamming Range** | 1.5 km (confirmed); up to 10.4 km demonstrated |
| **Jammer Frequency** | 20 MHz - 6,000 MHz |

### Deployment Variants

- **Xpeller Standard:** Vehicle-integrated or fixed-site installation
- **Xpeller Rapid:** Compact, rapidly deployable variant; combines radar, camera, RF detectors, and jammer in a single transport container or vehicle

### Operational Deployments

- GUARDION system for German Armed Forces (5 container-based drone defence systems for forward operating bases)
- Dutch Day of Remembrance in Amsterdam (May 2020)
- French heads-of-government meetings
- Airport protection (integrated with Frequentis)

## 6. Elysion C-UAS / C-UxS (Next-Generation Counter-UAS)

### Overview

Elysion C-UAS is HENSOLDT's next-generation fully integrated, modular counter-UAS solution, succeeding and expanding upon the Xpeller concept. It centres on the Elysion Mission Core C2 software with scalable sensor/effector configurations. In service since 2022 with the Bundeswehr as the ASUL system (developed by ESG, a HENSOLDT subsidiary).

### System Architecture

**Sensors (open interface architecture):**
- Active radar (e.g., Spexer 2000 3D)
- EO/IR cameras (e.g., NightOwl M)
- RF direction finders (e.g., Rohde & Schwarz)
- RF TDOA sensors
- Acoustic sensors
- AI image recognition
- Reconnaissance drones
- ADS-B receivers

**Effectors:**
- Smart RF jammers (soft kill)
- GPS/GNSS jammers (soft kill)
- High-Power Electro-Magnetics (HPEM) effector (soft kill)
- Net launchers (soft kill)
- Interceptor drones
- Kongsberg weapon station integration (hard kill, for ASUL)
- HP Wust effectors (hard kill, for ASUL)

**Command & Control - Elysion Mission Core:**
- Central C2 software with sensor data fusion
- AI-driven threat analysis and automated target prioritisation
- Automated decision-support with real-time action recommendations
- Single-operator capability
- Available in PC and tablet versions
- Swarm attack and electronic deception resilience

### Deployment Variants

- **Flexible Site Protection:** Tripod-based sensors/effectors for rapid deployment (e.g., airbase protection)
- **Shelterised Asset Protection:** Container-based system for deployment on military carrier vehicles; protection against sUAS under 25 kg
- **Vehicle-Integrated:** Mobile force protection

### Operational Status

- ASUL system in Bundeswehr service since 2022
- Deployed for force protection abroad and during major national/international events
- BAAINBw commissioned HENSOLDT for capability upgrade in May 2025
- Kinetic countermeasures integration (Kongsberg weapon station) underway

## 7. Twinvis Passive Radar

### Overview

Twinvis ("twin" + "invisible") is HENSOLDT's passive radar system that exploits existing broadcast transmitters (FM radio, DAB/DAB+, DVB-T/DVB-T2) to detect and track airborne targets without emitting any signals. It is inherently covert, jam-resistant, and capable of detecting low-observable (stealth) aircraft. Notably, HENSOLDT claimed it tracked a US F-35 stealth fighter departing ILA Berlin in 2018.

### Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Operating Principle** | Passive (exploits VHF/UHF broadcast signals) |
| **Signal Sources** | Up to 16 FM transmitters + 5 DAB/DAB+ frequencies + DVB-T/DVB-T2 |
| **Detection Range** | Up to 250 km radius |
| **Coverage** | Instantaneous 360 degrees |
| **Tracking Capacity** | >180 objects in 3D |
| **Maximum Tracked Targets** | ~200 aircraft simultaneously |
| **Update Rate** | 1 second |
| **Emission** | None (fully passive) |

### Key Features

- Invisible to radar warning receivers (no emissions)
- Jam-resistant (no active transmitter to jam)
- 3D omnidirectional tracking
- Real-time data fusion capability
- Low power consumption; no moving parts; minimal maintenance
- Deployable in clusters for extended coverage
- Low-frequency operation enhances detection of stealth coatings (less effective against VHF/UHF wavelengths)
- Gap filler / sensitive spot coverage
- Near-range high-precision slow-and-low target tracking

### Deployable Variant (Twinvis Sheltered)

- Can be mounted on military carrier vehicles
- Set up and dismantled by 2 people
- Self-sufficient operation possible without on-site personnel
- Includes high-performance antenna unit plus processor and infrastructure components
- Multiple customers under contract (inside and outside NATO)

### Civil Aviation Use

- HENSOLDT is working with DFS (German air navigation service provider) to certify Twinvis for civil air traffic use, target certification by end of 2026
- FAA demonstration completed in Miami, FL (CRADA partnership)
